Elk

Cervus canadensis

The elk ( Cervus canadensis) or wapiti [1] is one of the largest species within the deer family, Cervidae , and one of the largest terrestrial mammals in its native range of North America and Central and East Asia. The word "elk" originally referred to the European variety of the moose, Alces alces, but was transferred to Cervus canadensis by North American colonists. The name "wapiti", derived from a Shawnee and Cree word meaning "white rump", is also used for C. canadensis.
